# Adam James Tulsa - Design Principles

## The Feeling We Want
When someone visits adamjamestulsa.com, they should feel:
- Like they're meeting a friend who understands struggle
- Hope that change is possible
- Trust through transparency
- Pride in Tulsa community
- Welcomed, not sold to

## Visual Style Guide

### Colors (Use These Exact Ones)
- Primary: #8B4513 (Warm Brown - Tulsa earth)
- Secondary: #87CEEB (Sky Blue - mental health awareness)
- Accent: #87A96B (Sage Green - growth)
- Background: #FAF9F6 (Soft White)
- CTA Buttons: #1B3A57 (Deep Navy)
- Text: #2C3E50 (Soft Black)

### Fonts
- Headlines: Playfair Display or serif (storytelling feel)
- Body: Inter or sans-serif (easy reading)
- Don't use more than 2 fonts

### Images and Videos
- Real people from Tulsa (no stock photos)
- Warm, natural lighting
- Show Tulsa landmarks in backgrounds
- Video should show Adam speaking directly to camera

### Layout Rules
- Mobile-first (design for phones, then make bigger)
- Lots of white space (don't cram things)
- Curved corners on boxes (border-radius: 12px)
- Soft shadows (box-shadow: 0 4px 6px rgba(0,0,0,0.1))
- Big buttons for easy tapping (min 44px height)

### Words We Use
- "We" and "Us" not "I" and "Me"
- "Journey" not "Services"
- "Partners" not "Clients"
- "Tulsa families" not "Customers"
- "Second chances" not "Opportunities"

### Trust Signals to Include
- 500+ Families Housed
- 1,000+ Youth Supported
- 3 Thriving Businesses
- Real testimonials with photos
- Tulsa landmarks and references

### Things to Avoid
- Stock photos of generic people
- Corporate jargon
- Sharp edges and corners
- Cold gray colors
- Tiny text (min 16px)
- Overwhelming animations
